\section{End-to-End Example}

\begin{frame}{Modern Supply Chains: Optimization, ML, and Data Complexity}

Modern supply chains rely on both \textbf{mathematical optimization} and \textbf{machine learning} systems. Examples include:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Truck planning / allocation models} that optimize transportation resources under cost, capacity, and service constraints
  \item \textbf{ML classifiers} that determine whether products are eligible for air or ground transportation based on chemical composition and other product attributes
  \item \textbf{Forecasting models} that predict future demand, inventory needs, and operational trends
\end{itemize}

Decision-relevant information is often spread across:
\begin{itemize}
  \item databases, dashboards, and planning systems
  \item documentation, audit logs, and operational reports
  \item model artifacts, training data, and deployment pipelines
\end{itemize}

\begin{block}{Role of the Agentic Chatbot}
An agentic chatbot can \textbf{democratize access} to these systems by allowing even non-technical users to inspect models, query data, run analyses, and understand recommendations through natural language.
\end{block}

\end{frame}


\begin{frame}{What the Assistant Can Do}

The assistant can answer questions such as:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Which nodes are at highest stockout risk this week?
  \item Based on the model behavior and training data and audit, what are your \textbf{recommendations to improve the classifier}?
  \item If I change the optimization inputs, how will the \textbf{truck allocation} change?
  \item Can you run a \textbf{what-if analysis} with different capacity, demand, or cost assumptions?
  \item Can you trigger \textbf{retraining and redeployment} of the ML model under approval?
  \item How would forecast changes impact downstream transportation or inventory decisions?
  \item Can you summarize which assumptions are most responsible for the current planning output?
\end{itemize}

\begin{block}{Key value}
The value is not only faster and easier access to model outputs; it is the ability to discover why the model is wrong, where reality is richer than the formulation, and how to continuously close that \textbf{fidelity gap}.
\end{block}

\end{frame}


\begin{frame}{Mapping the Example to Agentic AI Concepts}

\midrule

\textbf{Skills} & Misclassification analysis, what-if simulation, forecasting diagnostics, recommendation generation, mitigation planning \\
\textbf{Memory} & Prior defects, planner overrides, historical disruptions, past model versions, earlier recommendations \\
\textbf{Guardrails} & Mask sensitive data, block unsafe actions, require approval for retraining/redeployment, log actions for auditability \\

\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\small\textit{The chatbot becomes agentic when it can reason across multiple sources, use tools, remember context, and act under guardrails.}

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{From Chatbot to Always-On Autonomous Agents}

\begin{block}{From user-driven interaction to persistent ownership}
The chatbot assists a human when asked.  
An always-on, self-evolving, long-running autonomous agent continuously \textbf{monitors}, \textbf{improves}, and \textbf{maintains} the systems over time.
\end{block}

\vspace{0.4em}
\textbf{Example instruction to an autonomous agent:}
\begin{quote}
\small
``Hi Agent, you own the supply-chain processes from now on. Maintain and improve the current state of the systems, keep them up to date as the world evolves, review the scientific literature, run experiments to improve performance, interact with senior domain experts and operations teams in the physical world, incorporate new business initiatives into our models and systems, and proactively identify opportunities for expansion. You are equipped with tools and a coding agent: you may use existing tools, update them, or build new ones when needed. You may also coordinate with other agents or teams as part of the workflow. Make decisions under uncertainty within guardrails and permission policies''
\end{quote}

\vspace{0.4em}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Always-on} rather than session-based
  \item \textbf{Self-improving} through experiments, monitoring, and feedback loops
  \item \textbf{Long-running} across changing business and operational conditions
  \item \textbf{Proactive} rather than only reactive
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}
